Our Goal

Changing Chicken's Lives

    Many people often eat their food, not once thinking about where it came from. We all know beef, pork, and chicken come from a farm somewhere off in the fields, but what we don’t know are the horrors and relevancy of slaughterhouse cruelty. In the United States, billions of chickens are killed, used for their eggs, and abused in total confinement. At slaughterhouses, especially corporate chicken farms, chickens are bred for flesh and drugged to expand natural sizes to be the most profitable. These abnormal weights will cause heart attacks, organ failure, and leg deformities, sometimes resulting in death because they are overweight and unable to reach water nozzles. Chickens are still alive when their throats are slit and when they’re scalded to death to remove their feathers because birds are exempt from the Humane Slaughter Act. These animals will never be with their families, root around in the soil, build nests, or do anything that is in their nature. Most won’t even see the sunlight until the day they’re loaded onto trucks headed for slaughterhouses.
    With Fight For Your Feathers, we hope to raise awareness about these horrible conditions that happen every day without public awareness. When you view the chicken as a person, you realize that animals are like people too, in which they have feelings, wants, and needs. When people say “Are you feeling like a little chicken?” they’re essentially calling the person a wimp, scared, and a coward. Fight For Your Feathers is a ultimately metaphor for people who feel “like a chicken” without their feathers. The chicken represents the person and the feathers represent courage, in which the chicken has been stripped from. Our overall message is that everyone deserves to feel happy and confident. By playing Fight For Your Feathers, we can all become aware of cruelty amongst chicken farms and ourselves as well.
